Problem Statement

Startup: Lumen Energy

They are on a mission to help commercial buildings substitute traditional energy consumption with Solar. Can this bring better economical benefits and a renewable standard?

Questions

In order to get a good understanding, we attempt to answer the following questions:


  1. What are the various factors that contribute to energy load in a building?
  2. Does weather drastically effect energy consumption of a building?
  3. Do identical building types produce similar energy load patterns?
  4. What features of a building affect the energy load?
Methods

Overall Framework

We sourced our project data with an initial dataset from LBNL, Berkeley. We received input data on Building features, energy load and weather. The datasets were cleaned of discrepancies; the missing values were predicted using forward fill and outliers were removed. The rest of the process is described below.


Weather Decomposition

After forming our training dataset,we performed weather boxplot to observe outliers and patterns in the datasets over the 4 years.

Time Series Decomposition

After forming our training dataset, we performed Time Series decomposition to observe the Trend, Seasonality and Residual error. Multiplicative decomposition does a finer job. Below we present for the year 2019 weather.

ARIMA Modelling

Auto Regression Integrated Moving Averages: This is a simultaneous linear regression performed on both the lagged values and forecast errors. Our model was already stationary through the ADF test, so we did not have to difference it (d=0). We performed autocorrelation to determine p,q values.


Results:Our MAPE value was about 22%. The fit could have been improved with larger training datasets. Prediction listed below.
